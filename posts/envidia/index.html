La Envidia No Es Sobre lo que el Otro Tiene, Sino Sobre lo que Creés que Te Falta

Por Lic. Camila Mastriaco | 21 de Enero, 2025 | Categoría: Autoestima

La envidia es una de las emociones más estigmatizadas y difíciles de admitir. Surge cuando deseamos algo que otra persona tiene, ya sea un logro, una cualidad o un bien material. Sin embargo, si la miramos de cerca, la envidia es menos sobre la otra persona y más sobre nosotros mismos. Es una mezcla de insatisfacción con lo que tenemos y admiración por los demás, y puede darnos pistas valiosas sobre nuestras propias metas y deseos.

Los Dos Tipos de Envidia

  • 'Healthy' envy or admiration: It arises as admiration for another's achievements. Instead of generating resentment, it can motivate us to improve, to strive harder, or to achieve our own goals. It is an envy that mobilizes us.
  • Envy with resentment: This is the most destructive form. It includes resentment towards the other person and can negatively affect our relationships and our self-esteem. It leaves us stuck in comparison and discomfort.

¿Por Qué Sentimos Envidia?

La envidia suele estar relacionada con:

  • Constant comparisons with others, especially in the age of social media.
  • Low self-esteem or insecurities that make us feel we are worth less than others.
  • Unrealistic expectations about life and what we 'should' have or have achieved.

Cómo Manejar la Envidia y Usarla a tu Favor

  1. Identify the origin: Instead of focusing on the person you envy, ask yourself: What exactly do I envy? Is it their professional success, their confidence, their relationship? The answer will show you what you long for in your own life.
  2. Change your perspective: Use envy as motivation. If you envy someone's physical fitness, take it as a sign to start taking better care of your health. Transform the 'I wish I had that' into 'what can I do to get closer to that?'.
  3. Be kind to yourself: Acknowledge your own achievements and your own path. Life is not a competition. Practicing gratitude for what you already have can significantly reduce feelings of lack.
  4. Talk about it: Sharing your feelings with someone you trust can help you process them better and realize that you are not alone in this emotion.
