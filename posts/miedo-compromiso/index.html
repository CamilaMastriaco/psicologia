Miedo al Compromiso: ¿Por Qué Algunas Personas Temen a las Relaciones Profundas?

Por Lic. Camila Mastriaco | 28 de Marzo, 2025 | Categoría: Relaciones

El compromiso no significa perder tu identidad, sino compartir tu vida sin dejar de ser vos mismo. Sin embargo, para muchas personas, la idea de establecer una relación estable y profunda genera un temor intenso. Este miedo al compromiso lleva a evitar vínculos serios o a sabotearlos inconscientemente cuando comienzan a avanzar, creando un ciclo de relaciones cortas y superficiales.

Posibles Causas del Miedo al Compromiso

Este temor no surge de la nada. Suele estar arraigado en experiencias y creencias profundas:

  • Painful past experiences: A betrayal, abandonment, or a toxic relationship in the past can create a protective shell.
  • Fear of losing freedom: Associating commitment with the loss of independence, personal time, or the ability to make one's own decisions.
  • Low self-esteem: Believing that one is not 'good enough' for a long-term relationship or that, eventually, the other person will realize it and leave.
  • Negative relationship models: Having grown up seeing conflictive or unhappy relationships in the family can generate a negative view of commitment.

¿Cómo Afecta a la Relación?

El miedo al compromiso se manifiesta a través de comportamientos como:

  • Evitación de conversaciones sobre el futuro.
  • Dudas constantes sobre la relación, incluso cuando todo va bien.
  • Autosabotaje: crear conflictos o distanciarse en momentos de cercanía emocional.

¿Cómo Empezar a Trabajarl?

  1. Identify and understand your fear: Where does it come from? What is the worst you think could happen if you commit? Naming the fear is the first step to disarming it.
  2. Work on trust and self-esteem: Often, the fear of commitment is a reflection of self-insecurity. Strengthening your self-esteem will help you feel more secure in any type of relationship.
  3. Learn to differentiate commitment from loss of freedom: A healthy relationship does not cancel your individuality, it enriches it. Commitment is a free choice to share a path, not a cage.
  4. Consider seeking professional help: If the fear is very intense and is sabotaging your happiness, therapy is a safe space to explore these wounds and build new ways of relating.
